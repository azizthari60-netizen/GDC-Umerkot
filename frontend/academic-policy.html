Academic Policy

Regulations governing attendance, assessment, promotion, and conduct for BS Chemistry students.

Program Duration & Credit Hours

The BS Chemistry program is a four-year undergraduate degree comprising eight semesters. Students must successfully complete the prescribed number of credit hours as per the approved curriculum to be eligible for the award of the degree.

Attendance Policy

  • Students are required to maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each course.
  • Shortfall in attendance may result in withholding of admit card or disqualification from final examination.
  • Medical or emergency leave must be documented and approved by the department.

Assessment & Grading

  • Continuous assessment includes quizzes, assignments, class participation, and mid-term examinations.
  • Final examination is conducted at the end of each semester as per the university schedule.
  • Weightage between sessional work and final examination follows the university's official policy.
  • Lab courses require successful completion of practical work, reports, and viva voce.

Laboratory Safety & Conduct

  • Students must wear appropriate lab coats, safety goggles, and follow all safety instructions.
  • Unauthorized experiments are strictly prohibited.
  • Chemical waste must be disposed of according to departmental guidelines.

Promotion & Retention

  • Students must meet the minimum CGPA requirement set by the university to be promoted to the next semester.
  • Failure in core courses must be cleared in subsequent semesters as per rules.
  • Academic probation and related actions follow university regulations.
